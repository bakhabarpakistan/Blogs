Case Study: Lessons from the Recent Pakistan vs India Clash

Posted on Sept 13, 2025

Cricket is more than just a sport in South Asia—it’s passion, pride, and identity. The recent Pakistan vs India match once again highlighted the striking contrast between the two teams, not just in terms of results but in terms of talent, structure, and mindset. This case study explores the key factors behind India’s rise, Pakistan’s struggles, and what needs to change if Pakistan wants to return to its glory days.

India’s Strengths: Talent, Depth, and Strategy

  • Power Hitting: India has built a pool of players who can hit consistently, whether it’s Rohit Sharma, Suryakumar Yadav, or Hardik Pandya.
  • Spin Department: Kuldeep Yadav and Axar Patel dominate the middle overs, taking wickets and controlling runs.
  • Fast Bowling: Jasprit Bumrah and Mohammed Siraj give India pace and accuracy Pakistan lacks.
  • Team Balance: India’s bench strength ensures continuity even when stars are absent.

Pakistan’s Struggles: Selection and Merit Issues

Pakistan continues to grapple with issues of selection and merit. The captain’s T20 stats don’t justify his leadership. Excluding match-winners like Babar Azam and Rizwan weakened the side. Unlike India, which trusted Kohli and Sharma to deliver, Pakistan sidelined its proven players.

Key Issues with Pakistan’s Approach

  • Lack of Merit: Favoritism in selection.
  • Poor Planning: Constant chopping and changing of squads.
  • Leadership Crisis: Captains without proven performance.
  • Neglecting Seniors: Experienced players pushed out instead of groomed as mentors.

Lessons from India

  • Consistency: Back your stars in major tournaments.
  • Domestic Development: Strong local structure for replacements.
  • Fitness & Discipline: Adopt strict standards like India.
  • Leadership: Captains must be top performers.

Conclusion

Pakistan cricket is at a crossroads. The recent clash was more than a defeat—it was a wake-up call. If Pakistan wants to compete, management must rebuild with honesty, stability, and merit. Otherwise, India will continue pulling further ahead while Pakistan lingers in inconsistency.
